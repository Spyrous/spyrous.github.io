Reading Material

This page is about educational material that I found useful, and I encourage you to take a look if you are interested in the topic.

Computer Architecture by Carnegie Mellon

The most organized course that I ever visited. It is targetting beginner/intermediate students and covers most of the topics that are present in the modern computer architecture. http://www.ece.cmu.edu/~ece447/s14/doku.php?id=schedule

Computer Architecture by Princeton

This course is not an introduction to computer architecture so be aware. You need to have completed an introductory course in computer architecture in order to get the best out of this course and to be able to understand what the course is about. I would say it’s a intermediate/advanced course covering more complex topics and you can find it for free on the Coursera platform.
https://www.coursera.org/course/comparch

Compilers by Stanford

Another free jewel made by Stanford’s professor Alex Aiken. While I am still at the lexical analysis part this course is really amazing as it covers the huge gap between the high level language to assembly code. The milestone of this course (that I wish to achieve in some future time) is to implement the COOL language and take all the steps to transform it from the high level to MIPS assembly.
https://www.coursera.org/course/compilers
